Nigerian rice and beans

Growing up, I always love to eat the Nigerian rice and beans and it ended up as my favorite dish to remind me of my home. It's a very delicious dish with aromatic scent that will send your taste buds to paradise. In this recipe, I will teach you how to make it so you can show off proudly to your family, friends, co-workers or anyone.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of rice
  • 2 cups of beans
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 3 tbsp canned tomatoes
  • 3 seasoning cubes
  • 1 cup of sliced onions
  • 2.2 lbs chicken
  • 1 tsp of powdered ginger
  • 1 tsp of powdered garlic
  • 2 cups of vegetable oil or any cooking oil
  • 5 scotch bonnet (peppers)
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

Instruction

  1. Start parboiling the rice but while you're doing that, pick and parboil the beans. Once the rice is done, wash both of them and place them in two different bowls.
  2. Grind the tomatoes (not the canned ones) with scotch bonnets and blend them together then parboil the chicken with all the Ingredients (2 seasoning cubes, 1/2 cups of sliced onions and salt) for 10-15 mins. Remove the chicken and set it aside (Don't forget to reserve the chicken stock).
  3. Fry or grill the chicken and set them aside.
  4. Transfer the beans into a pot and boil with water and sliced onions for 30-40 mins (30 mins for big white beans and 40 mins for brown beans). Makes sure that it's almost done.

Making Rice and Beans

Instruction

  1. Set the cooking pot on heat and add 1 cup of oil (The same oil that you used to fry the chicken). Add 1/2 cups of sliced onions, fry for 1 min and add ground tomatoes/pepper and canned tomatoes
  2. Add the chicken stock, 2 cups of water, 1 seasoning cube, salt, 1 ts each of curry, ginger, and garlic powder. Stir and let it boil.
  3. Add salt to taste, then add precooked bean and parboiled rice.
  4. Stir together and add remaining fried tomato/pepper that you previously removed. Spread evenly on top of the cooking rice and beans to let it cook properly before it burns. Add the remaining sliced onions.
  5. Cover the pot and let it cook on medium heat for next 25-40 mins. Makes sure to check time to time to see if the rice and beans are softened and the water is dried.
  6. Lastly, Use a wooden spoon to mix them together properly and serve with fried chicken. You can also add vegetable salad or fried plantains.

Ladies and Gentlemen, That's how you makes Nigerian rice and beans. I hope you and others enjoy the wonderful meal that you created.
